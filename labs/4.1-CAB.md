# Lab 4.1 – Change Advisory Board (CAB) Role Play

**Duration:** ~35 minutes  
**Format:** Group exercise — instructor-led role assignment

Your instructor will split you into groups and assign you one of the roles below before you begin.

| Role | Primary Concerns |
|------|-----------------|
| **Change Owner / Engineering Lead** | Justification, scope, testing completed, rollback plan |
| **CAB Chair / Release Manager** | Process completeness, scheduling, meeting facilitation |
| **Security & Compliance Officer** | CVE patching, access changes, audit trail, regulatory impact |
| **Operations / SRE Lead** | Maintenance window, rollback feasibility, on-call coverage, blast radius |
| **Business Stakeholder (Product Owner)** | Customer impact, timing relative to business cycles, revenue risk |

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lab you will be able to:

- Evaluate a change request from multiple stakeholder perspectives
- Practice structured CAB meeting facilitation and the RFC presentation format
- Identify technical, operational, security, and business risks in a proposed change
- Make and document a formal go/no-go decision with clear rationale
- Recognize how role and incentives shape the approval process in real organizations

---

## Background

A Change Advisory Board (CAB) is the governance body responsible for reviewing, authorizing, and scheduling changes to production systems. CABs exist to reduce risk by bringing diverse perspectives together before a change goes live — engineering, operations, security, compliance, and the business all have different concerns, and a change that looks low-risk from one angle may be high-risk from another.

In practice, CAB meetings are where priorities collide. Engineering wants to ship. Operations wants stability. Security wants auditability. The business wants no surprises during peak periods. Learning to navigate these tensions — and to make a well-reasoned decision under pressure — is a core skill for anyone involved in software delivery.

This lab simulates a real CAB meeting. You will take on a role, review a formal change request, ask hard questions, assess risk, and vote.

---

## Roles

The instructor assigns one role per person before the exercise begins. In groups smaller than 5, combine the **CAB Chair** with another role, or merge the two stakeholder roles (Security and Operations) into one. In larger groups, duplicate roles are fine — multiple people can represent the same perspective and compare notes during the debrief.

| Role | Primary Concerns |
|------|-----------------|
| **Change Owner / Engineering Lead** | Justification, scope, testing completed, rollback plan |
| **CAB Chair / Release Manager** | Process completeness, scheduling, meeting facilitation |
| **Security & Compliance Officer** | CVE patching, access changes, audit trail, regulatory impact |
| **Operations / SRE Lead** | Maintenance window, rollback feasibility, on-call coverage, blast radius |
| **Business Stakeholder (Product Owner)** | Customer impact, timing relative to business cycles, revenue risk |

> The CAB Chair facilitates the meeting. They do not advocate for or against the change — their job is to ensure the process runs correctly and that a decision is documented.

## The Change Request (RFC)

Read the following change request before the meeting begins.

---

**RFC-4471 — PostgreSQL Engine Upgrade: Production**

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Change ID** | RFC-4471 |
| **Change Type** | Normal |
| **Priority** | Medium |
| **Submitted By** | Platform Engineering Team |
| **Requested Window** | Saturday, 02:00–05:00 UTC |
| **Affected Systems** | Primary application database (`prod-db-01`), read replicas × 3 |
| **Change Summary** | Upgrade PostgreSQL engine from version 13.11 to 15.6 on the production RDS cluster |

**Justification:**  
PostgreSQL 13 reaches end-of-life in November. Version 15 includes security patches for 4 open CVEs flagged in the last security audit, improved query planner performance (~18% faster on benchmarks), and JSON path query support needed for the upcoming Q3 feature release.

**Testing Completed:**  
- Full regression suite executed against a staging replica — 847 tests passed, 3 skipped (unrelated to DB engine version per engineering assessment)  
- Load test run at 60% of peak production traffic — no degradation observed  
- Application compatibility review completed for ORM library (SQLAlchemy 1.4)

**Rollback Plan:**  
RDS snapshot taken immediately prior to upgrade window. In the event of a failed upgrade or application errors post-upgrade, the team will restore from snapshot. Estimated RTO: 25–40 minutes.

**Maintenance Window Impact:**  
The application will be unavailable during the upgrade window (estimated 45–75 minutes within the 3-hour window). A status page notification will be posted 48 hours in advance.

**Open Items:**  
- DBA team has not yet provided written sign-off (verbal approval received)  
- Load test was run at 60% capacity — peak traffic is typically 85–90% on Saturday mornings (later that same day)  
- The Q3 marketing campaign launches in 10 days; engineering estimates a 2-day buffer if rollback is needed

## Activity

Work through the following phases. The CAB Chair keeps time and moves the group forward.

### Phase 1 — Setup (5 minutes)

1. Your instructor has already assigned roles. Confirm everyone in your group knows their role.
2. Read the RFC above from your role's perspective. What stands out? What questions do you want to ask?
3. The CAB Chair confirms the agenda with the group: Presentation → Stakeholder Review → Risk Assessment → Vote → Document decision.

---

### Phase 2 — Change Owner Presentation (5 minutes)

The Change Owner presents the RFC to the CAB. Cover the following:

- What is changing and why now
- What testing has been completed and what was left out
- What the rollback plan is and how confident you are in it
- Any open items and your plan to close them

---

### Phase 3 — Stakeholder Review (10 minutes)

Each role asks the Change Owner 1–2 questions. Use the prompts below as a starting point — go deeper if the answers raise new concerns.

**CAB Chair / Release Manager**
- The DBA sign-off is listed as verbal only. Is this sufficient to proceed, or is written sign-off a hard requirement in your organization's change policy?
- Is the 3-hour window enough? What happens if the upgrade runs long?

**Security & Compliance Officer**
- Which 4 CVEs are being patched? Have any of them been assessed as actively exploitable in your environment?
- Does the upgrade change any database user permissions or authentication configuration?

**Operations / SRE Lead**
- The rollback RTO is 25–40 minutes. Is on-call staffed for the full window? Who makes the call to initiate rollback, and at what threshold?
- The load test only covered 60% of peak traffic. What is your confidence level in behavior at 85–90%?

**Business Stakeholder (Product Owner)**
- The Q3 campaign launches in 10 days. What is the business impact if we need those 2 rollback days?
- Has the 48-hour advance notice to customers been approved by marketing/comms?

---

### Phase 4 — Risk Assessment (5 minutes)

As a group, complete the following risk matrix. For each risk, agree on a **Likelihood** (Low / Medium / High) and **Impact** (Low / Medium / High) rating, then note any mitigation in place.

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation in Place? |
|------|-----------|--------|----------------------|
| Upgrade fails mid-window, requiring rollback | | | |
| Application incompatibility not caught in staging | | | |
| Rollback takes longer than 40 minutes, extending outage | | | |
| Change overlaps with elevated Saturday morning traffic | | | |

> There are no strictly correct answers. The goal is to surface disagreement between roles and work toward a shared assessment.

---

### Phase 5 — Vote and Decision (5 minutes)

The CAB Chair facilitates a vote. Each role casts one vote:

| Option | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Approve** | Proceed as submitted this Saturday |
| **Approve with Conditions** | Proceed only if specific items are resolved first |
| **Defer** | Reschedule to a later window — specify when and why |
| **Reject** | Do not proceed — specify what would need to change |

After the vote, the CAB Chair documents the outcome:

**Decision:** _______________  
**Vote:** ___/5 in favor  
**Conditions or Rationale:** _______________  
**Open Items to Resolve Before Execution (if any):** _______________

---

### Phase 6 — Debrief (5 minutes)

Discuss as a group:

1. Which role felt the most pressure to approve? The most pressure to block? Why?
2. The DBA sign-off is only verbal. In your organization, would that be acceptable? What are the risks of treating verbal approval as sufficient?
3. The load test covered 60% of peak traffic. How would you decide whether that is "good enough"?
4. If the vote was split, what happens next? Who has final authority in a real CAB?
5. How does the 10-day campaign launch change the risk calculus — should that be a blocker, a condition, or irrelevant to the CAB?

In this lab you:

- Reviewed a real-format RFC and evaluated it from five different stakeholder perspectives
- Practiced structured CAB meeting facilitation with timed phases
- Completed a risk matrix and surfaced disagreements between roles
